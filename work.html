Initiative 01
Proprietary Customer Health Score

Our customer base and product are unique — off-the-shelf health scoring tools don't capture that complexity. We needed a system that could accurately reflect true customer health and give our CS team a clear, actionable north star.

92%+
Retention, Top Health Tier
The Problem
No existing tool could account for the nuances of our customer base. Health signals needed to flex based on customer type and contract size to be meaningful — a one-size-fits-all score would misrepresent risk across our portfolio.
What Was Built
An in-house Customer Health Score combining product usage, engagement signals, and sentiment data. The scoring model dynamically adjusts its weights based on customer segment and contract size, ensuring accuracy at every tier of the business.
The Outcome
CS teams can instantly prioritize their accounts and identify the right intervention for each health tier. The score eliminated guesswork, reduced time spent on manual account reviews, and became the CS team's primary operating mechanism. Customers in the top health tier sustain 92%+ retention rates consistently.
Initiative 02
Bottoms-Up GTM Operating Model

We needed a plan for the upcoming fiscal year — not just a top-down revenue number, but a model the entire leadership team could trust, stress-test, and operate against.

The Problem
Annual planning lacked granularity. Leadership had a revenue target but no clear line of sight into what inputs — leads, conversion rates, headcount, territory mix — would actually get us there.
What Was Built
A bottoms-up model projecting Bookings, Churn, and Net Revenue — built from first principles. The model captures expected leads by source, lead-to-MQL rate, MQL-to-SQL rate by source, Opp-to-Close rate by source and team, ASP by region and team, and retention rates across 6 distinct customer cohorts.
Result
Became the cornerstone for in-year tracking, giving leadership a living benchmark to evaluate performance against plan at every stage of the funnel.
Initiative 03
Flawless CS Re-Organization

After determining that a Pod structure would better serve customers and drive improved outcomes, we needed to execute a major operational change — without disrupting 150+ customer relationships in the process.

The Problem
Structural changes in CS almost always create customer risk. We needed to redesign territory alignments, reassign accounts across CSM and AM Pods, and establish entirely new cohorts — all while maintaining continuity and trust with our customer base.
What Was Built
New ACV-based cohorts and territory alignments across the full customer base. Strategic account-to-CSM/AM Pod matching to preserve relationship continuity. A transition playbook that led every conversation with customer value — not internal operational language.
Result
150+ customer transitions completed in 3 months. One piece of negative feedback. Zero transition-related churn.
